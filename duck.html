Ducks

Ducks, also called waterfowl, are a category of bird. Ducks are traditionally divided among three subfamilies: the monotypic surface-feeders, the sea ducks and the freshwater ducks. Although they have many different characteristics, one trait all ducks share is that they dive for their food. Ducks are highly adapted to living in the water. They have webbed feet and their feathers are oily, which gives them waterproofing and helps keep them warm. Ducks are found all over the world, and there are dozens of different species.

Ducks are social animals and live in small groups or large flocks. Ducks are very protective of their young and will even attack predators if they feel their offspring are in danger. Male ducks are called drakes and the females are called hens. Both drakes and hens have a bill that is broad and flat. Their diet consists mostly of plants, but they will also eat small insects, fish and other small animals.

Types of Ducks

There are many different types of ducks, but they can generally be divided into three categories: surface-feeders, sea ducks and freshwater ducks. Some of the most popular types of ducks include the mallard, the Muscovy duck, the wood duck and the Mandarin duck.

Mallard

The mallard is one of the most popular types of ducks and is also the ancestor of most domestic ducks. Mallards are surface-feeders and are often found in parks and other urban areas. Male mallards have a bright green head, while the females are more brown in color. Mallards can be found in many different parts of the world and are the mostcommon type of duck in North America.

Muscovy Duck

The Muscovy duck is a large duck that is native to South America. Male Muscovy ducks can weigh up to 8 pounds and have a wingspan of up to 6 feet. These ducks are often considered to be pests in some parts of the world because of their size and their tendency to invade agricultural areas. Muscovy ducks are not as popular as some of the other types of ducks, but they are still widely considered to be a delicacy in many parts of the world.

Wood Duck

The wood duck is a type of freshwater duck that is native to North America. Wood ducks are smaller than some of the other types of ducks and have a more slender body. Male wood ducks are very colorful and have a bright red bill. Wood ducks are not as common as some of the other types of ducks, but they are still widely hunted for their meat and their eggs.

Mandarin Duck
